Venti

The Tall Grande and Venti nomenclature at Starbucks is derived from the Italian language. The word "Tall" refers to the size of the drink, "Grande" means large, and "Venti" means twenty. This naming convention was established in order to provide clarity for customers ordering drinks in a foreign language.. Everything You Need to Know About Starbucks Coffee Sizes Venti. Here's where we veer off course a little further because a "Venti" size at Starbucks means 20 ounces for hot drinks and 24 for cold. ("Venti" means "20" in Italian.) The Venti is equivalent to a large in "standard" sizing.

Ingredients:

  1. Espresso shots: Typically, a Venti-sized latte contains two shots of espresso.
  2. Steamed milk: Whole milk is commonly used for lattes, but you can also choose non-dairy alternatives like almond or soy milk.
  3. Flavor syrup (optional): If you want to add flavor to your latte, you can choose from a variety of syrups available at Starbucks, such as vanilla, caramel, hazelnut, etc.

How Is It Made?

Go to the Starbucks counter and ask for a Venti Latte.Specify any flavor syrup you'd like, if desired.The barista will pull two shots of espresso and steam the milk.The espresso shots will be poured into your Venti-sized cup.The steamed milk will be added to the cup, creating a creamy texture.If you opted for flavor syrup, it will be added to the cup before the milk.Optionally, the barista may add a decorative swirl or foam design on top of your latte.Your Venti Latte will be served to you hot and ready to enjoy.
